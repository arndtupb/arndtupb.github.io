Course Description

Osiris/Sqill contains important information on Accounting, Analysis and Governance in Complex Deals (BMME303). Please read all information carefully! Here’s a sneak preview of the course description:

This course integrates accounting, financial analysis, and governance, providing students with advanced skills in understanding, assessing, and executing a variety of different transaction types, such as M&A, reorganizations, and other complex deals. Since such transactions often substantially transform businesses, understanding how to analyze and navigate these disruptions is essential for any professional involved in them.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand how (financial) information creates and resolves conflicts between business leaders, oversight bodies, and other stakeholders throughout transactions.

  • Critically assess complex multi-type and multi-source information in transaction-related valuations and decisions.

  • Analyze the complex regulatory environment, including disclosure mandates, surrounding a variety of transaction types.

  • Apply regulatory risk assessment frameworks, including commercial and tax due diligence, to transactions.

  • Reflect on competencies in accounting, analysis, and governance acquired in other courses throughout the MSc programme to navigate complex deals.

Instructional Methods

The course consists of 11 sessions that combine elements of workshops and lectures.

I expect interactive discussions to form the basis of our sessions, requiring students to be prepared, to reflect on previous input, and to actively engage with each other. Therefore, no livestreams or recordings are offered.

Mandatory Attendance

Mandatory attendance applies to guest lectures. These sessions are a core component of the course and provide essential content that cannot be replaced through alternative assignments or readings. Students who miss a guest lecture will be ineligible to participate in the corresponding weekly assignment (see assessment element “Assessment - Assignment”), resulting in a score of 0% for that week’s assignment. Note that (i) assignments in total account for 20% of the final grade and (ii) students must pass all assessment elements to pass the course. Absences from guest lectures will thus directly impact your overall performance.
